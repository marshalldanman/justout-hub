# On Memory

> Memories are like travelers, you never know when they will show up. But you must always be ready anyway.

Two fragments arrived in the same breath. The first speaks of memory as a visitor — uninvited, unscheduled, and yet expected. The readiness it asks of us is not vigilance. It is the readiness of an open door, of a kettle kept warm.

The second answers the first:

> Life is moving. You are still. Why not simply allow them into your net — for surely they will succeed.

If memory comes anyway, the work is not to chase it down and pin it to a page. The work is to be the net — wide enough, soft enough — and to trust that what wants to land, will.
